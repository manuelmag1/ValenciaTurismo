Las Fallas

Date: March 15-19 | Location: City Center, Valencia

Las Fallas is the most important festival in Valencia and one of Spain's most spectacular celebrations. This ancient tradition, dating back centuries, transforms the city into an open-air gallery of impressive wooden sculptures called "fallas."

Each neighborhood creates elaborate monuments, some reaching up to 20 meters in height, depicting satirical scenes of current events and social issues. The streets fill with fireworks, parades, and celebrations day and night for five days.

The climax comes on March 19 when all fallas are set on fire simultaneously in "la Cremà" (The Burning) - a magical, chaotic, and unforgettable spectacle that lights up the night sky.

La Tomatina

Date: Last Wednesday of August | Location: Buñol (30km from Valencia)

La Tomatina is the world's largest food fight festival, where thousands of people gather in the streets of Buñol to throw over 150,000 kilos of ripe tomatoes at each other. It's a chaotic, joyful, and utterly unique celebration that attracts visitors from around the globe.

The festival begins when participants climb a ham pole to grab a hanging ham, signaling the start of one hour of tomato-throwing madness. The streets become rivers of red pulp, and everyone gets completely drenched in tomato juice.

At the end, fire trucks wash the streets clean with their hoses. It's not just about the mess - it's a celebration of abundance, fun, and community spirit that has made Buñol famous worldwide.

Valencia Marathon

Date: December 1st | Location: Throughout the City

The Valencia Marathon is known as "The City of Running" event and ranks among the fastest and most beautiful marathons in the world. Run on a completely flat course with perfect conditions, thousands of runners from every continent gather to participate.

The 42.195km route takes participants through Valencia's most iconic locations, including the stunning Turia Gardens - a 9km linear park built on a former riverbed that winds through the entire city.

The marathon attracts elite runners and recreational athletes alike. The combination of Valencia's pleasant weather, enthusiastic crowds, and architectural beauty makes this one of the most memorable marathon experiences in the world.

Gran Feria - Batalla de Flores

Date: July 28th | Location: Paseo de la Alameda, Valencia

The Battle of Flowers (Batalla de Flores) is the grand finale of Valencia's summer fair and one of the most beautiful parades in the world. Elaborate floats decorated with millions of fresh flowers move through the streets of Valencia while crowds throw flowers at each other.

This unique festival blends the traditions of Spanish fairs with an explosion of color and natural beauty. The main parade takes place along Paseo de la Alameda, where specially designed floats carry people dressed in traditional and contemporary costumes, showering the crowd with flowers.

The atmosphere is magical - the air fills with petals, the fragrance of fresh flowers surrounds you, and the joy is infectious. It's a celebration of summer, nature, and the artistic spirit of Valencia that leaves visitors mesmerized.
